# Session Log: 2026-02-28

Last updated: 2026-02-28

## Goal

Build a working submission for the AdderBoard challenge: smallest transformer
that adds two 10-digit numbers with >= 99% accuracy on 10K held-out pairs.

## What Happened

### Track 1a: Hand-Coded 2L Model -- SUCCESS (249 params)

Built a 2-layer decoder with analytically set weights. 100% accuracy on all
10,010 test cases. 249 total parameters.

**Architecture**: 2L decoder, d_model=5, 2 heads (d_head=2), ff=4 per layer.

**Key design decisions**:

1. **Input format**: Reversed digits (LSB-first) with separator tokens.
   `a0 a1 ... a9 SEP b0 b1 ... b9 SEP` (22 tokens).

2. **Fixed attention routing** (0-param buffers): Head 0 gathers A_i + B_i,
   Head 1 gathers A_{i-1} + B_{i-1}. No Q/K projections needed.

3. **Carry detection via clamped ReLU step**:
   diff = (A_{i-1}+B_{i-1}) - S_{i-1} is always in {-1, 0, 9, 10}.
   carry = ReLU(diff - 0.5) - ReLU(diff - 1.5) gives exact {0, 1}.

4. **Mod-10 via two-hinge ReLU**:
   step(S>=10) = ReLU(2S-19) - ReLU(2S-20) is exact for integer S.
   Result = S - 10*step.

5. **Parabolic LM head**: logit[c] = -(x - c - 0.5)^2 peaks at correct digit.

**Critical insight proved during development**: A linear LM head CANNOT perform
modular-10 decode for inputs [0, 19]. This means mod-10 MUST be computed in the
MLP. With carry-in-MLP, this requires 2 layers to chain carry -> mod10.

### Track 1b: Hand-Coded 1L Model -- SUCCESS (33 unique params)

After analyzing Gemini's working v2 submission (also 33 params), built an
independent 1-layer implementation incorporating the key insight we missed:
**carry detection belongs in attention, not the MLP**.

**Architecture**: 1L decoder, d_model=3, 3 heads (d_head=1), ff=4.

**Key techniques**:

1. **ALiBi prefix sum for carry**: Head 1 uses scores -(k-j)*log(10) to produce
   base-10 exponential weights. The prefix sum S_k encodes carry as floor(S_k).

2. **Residual cancellation**: Head 2 (V = -token_value) cancels the residual
   connection, giving the MLP clean (A+B) regardless of previous output.

3. **e^80 softmax anchoring**: Separator token gets score 80.0. O_proj multiplies
   by e^80 to recover exact values. Error ~3.6e-35 vs 1e-11 thresholds.

4. **2-hinge ReLU**: Paired neurons with epsilon offset (1e-11 vs 0.5e-11) give
   exact 0/1 step functions. Scaled by 2e11 -> +1 carry, 2e12 -> -10 overflow.

**Result**: 10,010/10,010 (100%), 33 unique params, QUALIFIED.

### Track 2: Trained Model -- FAILED (2.5% max accuracy)

Architecture: 1L decoder, d_model=7, 1 head, ff=14 (777 params).
Curriculum training (3 phases) + extended grokking phase.
107K steps total, loss dropped from 1.92 to 0.38 but accuracy never exceeded
2.5%.

**Why it failed**:
- Missing rank-3 factorization (all successful trained models use this)
- No weight tying between embedding and LM head
- Likely needs much longer training or different optimizer settings

## Key Architectural Lesson

The fundamental difference between 249 params and 33 params is WHERE carry
detection happens:

- **Carry in MLP** (our 2L model): Layer 1 attention gathers current and
  previous digit pairs. Layer 1 MLP detects carry by comparing previous output
  to previous pair sum. Layer 2 MLP computes mod-10. Requires 2 layers.

- **Carry in attention** (1L model): Head 1 uses ALiBi base-10 prefix sum to
  compute carries across all preceding positions in one attention pass.
  MLP only needs to extract carry (+1) and detect overflow (-10). 1 layer.

The impossibility proof (linear head can't do mod-10) is correct but was
applied too broadly: it rules out deferring mod-10 to the LM head, but both
models compute mod-10 in the MLP. The proof should have told us "compute mod-10
before the head" not "use 2 layers."

## Reflections

- The carry-in-attention insight is the single most impactful architectural
  decision. It reduces layers from 2 to 1 and d_model from 5 to 3.

- The residual cancellation trick (Head 2 with V = -token) is a reusable
  pattern for hand-coded transformers where the residual interferes with MLP
  computation.

- e^80 softmax anchoring is more robust than initially assumed. The 24
  orders of magnitude safety margin makes it practically exact.

- Testing remains the strongest predictor of success. Gemini v1 (no testing)
  scored 0%. Every model that tested (Claude 2L, Claude 1L, Gemini v2) scored
  100%.
